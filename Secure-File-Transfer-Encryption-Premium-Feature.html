Frequently Asked Questions

How does transfer encryption work?

When you enable transfer encryption, your files are encrypted on your device using AES-256 encryption before being sent to our servers. The encrypted data is then transferred to the destination cloud service. Only you hold the decryption keys, ensuring that no one else can access your files during transfer.

Will encryption slow down my transfers?

Our encryption implementation is highly optimized and typically adds minimal overhead to your transfers. For most users, the difference in transfer speed is negligible, especially when balanced against the significant security benefits.

Can I use encryption with all cloud services?

Yes, our transfer encryption works with all supported cloud storage providers including Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, and the premium providers Box and iCloud. The encryption process happens before files are sent to any cloud service.

What happens if I lose my encryption key?

We utilize a zero-knowledge architecture, which means we don't store your encryption keys. It's essential to securely backup your keys. During setup, we provide options for key backup and recovery methods to prevent permanent data loss.

Is encrypted transfer compatible with mobile devices?

Yes, our encryption feature works seamlessly across all platforms including our mobile apps for iOS and Android. Your encrypted transfers maintain the same security standards regardless of the device you're using.
